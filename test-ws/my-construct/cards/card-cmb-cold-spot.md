## Summary
The CMB cold spot is a large, unusually cold region in the cosmic microwave background that may represent a statistical fluctuation or new physics.

## Key Points
- ~5° radius cold region in the southern hemisphere
- Significance varies by analysis method
- Possible explanations: supervoid, cosmic texture, statistical fluctuation
- Void lensing analysis needed to distinguish causes
