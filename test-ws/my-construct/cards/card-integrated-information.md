## Summary
Integrated Information Theory (Tononi, 2004) proposes that consciousness corresponds to the capacity of a system to integrate information, measured by Φ (phi).

## Key Points
- Φ measures the information generated by a system beyond its parts
- High Φ → conscious; low Φ → not conscious
- Makes testable predictions about neural correlates
- Implies panpsychist conclusions about simple systems with non-zero Φ
- Subject to debate about whether Φ captures the right property
