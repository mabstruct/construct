## Summary
Panpsychism is the view that consciousness is a fundamental and ubiquitous feature of the physical world, not an emergent property unique to complex organisms.

## Key Points
- Consciousness as fundamental property of matter
- Avoids the emergence problem of physicalism
- Faces the combination problem (how micro-experiences combine)
- Modern forms: constitutive panpsychism, Russellian monism
- Connected to IIT through shared intuitions about distribution
