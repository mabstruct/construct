## Summary
The hard problem of consciousness (Chalmers, 1995) is the problem of explaining why and how physical processes give rise to subjective experience (qualia).

## Key Points
- Distinct from the 'easy problems' of cognitive function
- Easy problems are explainable through functional/causal roles
- Hard problem resists functional explanation
- Even complete neuroscientific description leaves the 'what it is like' unexplained
- Central to the explanatory gap between physical and phenomenal
