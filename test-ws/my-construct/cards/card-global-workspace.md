## Summary
Global Workspace Theory (Baars, 1988) proposes that consciousness functions as a global workspace — a central information exchange that broadcasts selected content to many specialized processors.

## Key Points
- Unconscious processors compete for access to the global workspace
- Conscious content is what gains access and gets broadcast
- Explains limited capacity and integration of conscious experience
- Supported by fMRI and EEG studies showing late global ignition
- Extended by Dehaene's Global Neuronal Workspace (GNW) model
