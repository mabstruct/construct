## Summary
The zombie argument (Chalmers, 1996) argues that if a philosophical zombie — a being physically identical to a conscious human but lacking subjective experience — is conceivable, then consciousness is non-physical.

## Key Points
- P-zombie is physically identical but has no qualia
- Conceivability of P-zombies → metaphysical possibility (per Chalmers)
- If P-zombies are possible, physicalism is false
- Criticism: conceivability does not entail metaphysical possibility
- Response: phenomenal concepts have unique epistemic properties
