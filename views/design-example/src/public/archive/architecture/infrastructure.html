Data Model

CONSTRUCT organizes knowledge in three hierarchical layers: topics as regions, tags as landmarks within those regions, and connections that weave them together.

Layer 1: Topics (Regions)
Broad research domains that organize knowledge into manageable clusters. Examples: "robot-manipulation", "intent-understanding", "semantic-search".
"topic": "robot-manipulation",
"description": "Techniques for grasping and moving objects",
"created": "2024-02-24"
Layer 2: Tags (Landmarks)
Specific research areas within topics, each with a semantic weight indicating relevance. Tags have hierarchical relationships and are embedded for semantic search.
"tag": "gripper-kinematics",
"topic": "robot-manipulation",
"weight": 1.8,
"embedding": [0.15, -0.42, ...],
"last_updated": "2024-03-14"
Layer 3: Connections (Relationships)
Semantic relationships between tags that capture how knowledge domains relate. Types include: extends (builds upon), contradicts (opposing views), parallels (analogous concepts), grounds (theoretical foundation), cites (references), relates (general connection).
"from_tag": "gripper-kinematics",
"to_tag": "deep-reinforcement-learning",
"type": "extends",
"evidence_count": 3,
"confidence": 0.87
